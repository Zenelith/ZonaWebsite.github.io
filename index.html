Legal issues

Data protection act

The data protection act is an act that governs the use of data, more specifically customer data. this act puts forward rules in relation to processing customer data, making sure the information is kept secure and processed for a specific purpose. Companies are made to follow this legislation in order to keep customer information safe and secure.

Computer misuse act

The Computer misuse act is designed to protect users from unlawful attacks and theft of information, this is an act brought into play during issues relating to hackers stealing information from customers this act additionally covers unauthorised access to a system, making those found guilty prosecuted. This alongside the data protection act secures customer data and general data from thieves

Ethical Issues

Codes of practices when using email

Many organisations enforce codes of practice when using email, be it how much information can be shared under certain acts or even company policy. However most email standards revolve around do not send harassing emails or use emails unlawfully. As emails in a business should only be used to send information and data or respond to clients.

Internet Whistle-blower

Internet whistleblowing is when someone is anonymously reported for misuse or unlawful use, this mostly takes shape in the form of employees who may call out other employees for misuse to system administrators. It is important there are guidelines in place for Internet whistle blowing or any form of anonymous report including a report threshold i.e what you can and cannot report someone for.

Operational Issues

Impact of increasing sophisticated systems

The operational issue around the impact of increasing sophisticated systems mostly revolves around automation putting some out of jobs. However today software is now more advantaged than what it was, meaning more trained personnel are required for operation. and when the software has an issue it can send a business into chaos requiring a large amount of maintenance and downtime

Security of information

Security of information is crucial to a business, it enforces that data is safe from unauthorised or unlawful access and ensures a business does not violate any acts protecting information such as the Data protection act, meaning a business should have a management system inplace dictating who has access to information for iteration and revisions.
